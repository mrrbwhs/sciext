4.1 – What are the differences and similarities between scientific theories and laws?
  • 4.1.1 – collect primary data to investigate the law of conservation of mass
  • 4.1.2 – collect secondary-sourced data to investigate the theory of plate tectonics
  • 4.1.3 – compare the characteristics of theories and laws
4.2 – What leads to a theory being developed?
  • 4.2.1 – gather secondary-sourced data to investigate the supporting evidence and development of theories, including but not limited to:
    • 4.2.1.1 – germ theory
    • 4.2.1.2 – oxygen theory of combustion
  • 4.2.2 – gather secondary-sourced data to investigate how aspects of a theory can be disproved through the collection of evidence, including:
    • 4.2.2.1 – Geocentric Theory (of the solar system)
    • 4.2.2.2 – Theory of Inheritance of Acquired Characteristics
    • 4.2.2.3 – Dalton’s atomic theory
    • 4.2.2.4 – Steady State Theory of the Universe (in cosmology)
4.3 – What leads to the acceptance of a scientific law?
  • 4.3.1 – gather secondary-sourced data to investigate and assess the evidence that supports scientific laws, including but not limited to:
    • 4.3.1.1 – Newton’s Second Law of Motion
    • 4.3.1.2 – Avogadro’s Law
    • 4.3.1.3 – law of superposition
    • 4.3.1.4 – Mendel’s Law of Dominance
  • 4.3.2 – design and collect primary data to show that results can be predicted by laws, including but not limited to:
    • 4.3.2.1 – Ohm’s Law
    • 4.3.2.2 – law of conservation of energy
4.4 – How are theories and laws used in science?
  • 4.4.1 – investigate how the law of conservation of energy is applied in different science disciplines through primary and secondary-sourced research, including but not limited to:
    • 4.4.1.1 – Chemistry
    • 4.4.1.2 – Physics
    • 4.4.1.3 – Human Biology
    • 4.4.1.4 – Earth and Environmental Science
  • 4.4.2 – demonstrate, using evidence and examples, how diverse phenomena have been unified into specific theories, for example:
    • 4.4.2.1 – atomic theory
    • 4.4.2.2 – theory of evolution
    • 4.4.2.3 – Big Bang theory
    • 4.4.2.4 – plate tectonic theory
  • 4.4.3 – gather secondary-sourced data to investigate how scientific investigations of nuclear reactions and decay changed the way in which the law of conservation of mass and law of conservation of energy are interpreted
